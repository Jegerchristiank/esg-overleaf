A) Kort bogprofil  
"A World Of Standards" udforsker betydningen af standardisering i samfundet og dens indflydelse på organisationer, governance og sociale normer. Bogen analyserer, hvordan standarder skaber orden og regulering i en globaliseret verden, og hvordan de påvirker både private og offentlige aktører. Gennem en dybdegående undersøgelse af ekspertviden og organisatoriske strukturer belyser den de komplekse dynamikker, der ligger til grund for standardiseringens udvikling og implementering.

B) Temaer  
Standardization: Standardisering påvirker organisationer og skaber samfundsmæssige regler. Den fremmer globalt samarbejde og ensartethed, men kan også føre til uheldige konsekvenser.  
Organizational Influence: Organisationers handlinger formes af deres tro og tilpasning til samfundets regler. De spiller en central rolle i at producere og implementere standarder.  
Expert Knowledge: Ekspertviden er afgørende for standarder, men kan også true professionel ekspertise. Der er ofte uenighed bag facaden af ekspertise.  
Uniformity: Standarder skaber ensartethed, hvilket kan påvirke mode og design. Processer til at opnå ensartethed er essentielle for standardisering.  
Governance: Standarder fungerer som reguleringsmekanismer i samfundet. De kan være bindende eller ikke-bindende og spiller en rolle i international governance.  
Quality and Procedures: Kvalitetsstandarder fokuserer på processer frem for resultater. Procedurerne er ofte mere vigtige end de faktiske udfald i standardisering.  

C) Meta-principper  
Vigtigheden af standarder: Standarder er essentielle for governance og koordinering i samfundet, hvilket understreger behovet for yderligere forskning.  
Organisationers rolle: Organisationer er centrale i skabelsen af samfundsmæssige regler, hvilket opfordrer til undersøgelse af deres processer.  
Interdisciplinær samarbejde: Samarbejde mellem forskellige sociale videnskaber forbedrer forskningskvaliteten.  
Regulering gennem standarder: Standarder fungerer som reguleringsmekanismer i forskellige felter, hvilket analyserer deres indflydelse på adfærd.  
Globalisering af standarder: Overgangen fra nationale til internationale standarder afspejler globaliseringens tendenser.  
Voluntary compliance: Standarder er ofte frivillige, men bredt accepterede, hvilket kræver forståelse af compliance-dynamikker.  
Kompleksitet i standardoprettelse: Oprettelse af standarder involverer mange faktorer og er sjældent en ligetil proces.  
Markedsdynamik: Standarder kan skabe markeder og påvirke forbrugeradfærd, hvilket udfordrer traditionelle marketingstrategier.  
Uafhængighed af aktører: Adoptere standarder skal have frihed til at vælge, hvilket understreger betydningen af agentur.  
Forventninger og adfærd: Regelmæssighed i handlinger fører til forudsigelige sociale processer, hvilket er vigtigt for forståelsen af interaktioner.

E) Begrebsbank  
Standardization: The process of establishing and applying rules or standards to ensure consistency and quality.  
Governance: The act of governing or overseeing the control and direction of an organization or society.  
Expert Knowledge: Specialized knowledge that informs the creation and application of standards.  
Uniformity: The state of being uniform or consistent across different entities or practices.  
Voluntary Standards: Guidelines that are not mandatory but are widely followed.  
Standardizers: Organizations or individuals that create and promote standards.  
INGOs: International non-governmental organizations that issue standards on diverse subjects.  
Soft Law: Non-binding agreements or recommendations issued by international organizations.  
Neo-voluntarism: A governance approach emphasizing voluntary compliance over mandatory regulations.  
Administrative Standards: Guidelines that dictate how organizational processes should be managed and controlled.  
Quality Standards: Criteria that outline the processes necessary to achieve high quality in products or services.  
Actors: Entities with the ability to act independently within a given context.  
Regime: A set of governing arrangements including rules, norms, and procedures.  
Institutionalization: The process by which certain ideas and patterns of action become taken for granted.  
Compliance: The act of conforming to established rules or standards set by an organization.  
Certification: The process of verifying that a product, service, or system meets specified standards.  
Lock-in Processes: Situations where initial choices limit future options due to interdependencies.  
Market Structure: The organizational characteristics of a market, influencing competition and behavior.  
Democratic Deficit: A situation where democratic institutions or processes are perceived as lacking accountability or representation.  
Interdependence: Mutual reliance between supply and demand in standardization.  
Individualization: The trend of individuals perceiving themselves as independent with unique norms and goals.  
Globalization: The process of increased interconnectedness and interaction among global actors.  
Knowledge Workers: Professionals who possess specialized expertise and operate through networks.  
Documentation: The act of creating records that provide evidence of compliance with standards.  
Impersonal Responsibility: The lack of individual accountability in the context of standards that are not tied to specific individuals.  
Technicalization: The reliance on technical expertise in governance and policy.  
ISO 9000: A set of international standards for quality management and assurance.  
Self-Regulation: The ability of an organization or industry to govern itself without external control.  
Social space: The absence of existing organizations that allows for the formation of new ones.  
Exclusivity: The quality of being limited to a select group, restricting broader participation.
